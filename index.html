Muscular System and Fibromyalgia

Welcome to our Stage 4 PIA Project website We are Team 3 from Group 217 at CIDEB, Universidad Autónoma de Nuevo León. In this project, we investigated the Muscular System one of the most essential systems in the human body, responsible for every movement we make, from breathing to running.

Our research focused on Fibromyalgia, a chronic condition characterized by widespread muscle pain, fatigue, and tenderness throughout the body. Although it doesn't directly damage the muscles, it alters how the brain processes pain signals, leading to constant discomfort and seriously impacting quality of life.

To address this problem, our team designed a robotic solution: a nano-bio-robot that travels through the bloodstream via the arteries to reach the affected muscle tissue and release analgesics in a controlled manner targeting only the painful area rather than numbing the entire body. This website documents our full research, programming, robotic design, and team journey throughout the semester.

Through this project we developed skills in programming, robotics, biology, and teamwork combining science and technology to propose an innovative solution to a real health problem. Our topic is the Muscular System and Fibromyalgia.

01 Research Data

The Muscular System

Muscle problems can affect many people and range from mild issues like cramps to serious chronic disorders. These conditions can interfere with daily movement, physical performance, and overall quality of life. Some muscle problems are temporary and easy to treat, while others are long-term and more complex, such as inflammatory muscle diseases and fibromyalgia. Understanding these problems is important because many symptoms can be similar, and correct identification helps choose the best treatment.

The muscular system is fundamental for movement and the overall functioning of the body, as it allows us to perform everything from basic activities to complex movements. It works together with the skeletal and nervous systems to produce coordinated action throughout the body.

02 Problems

Fibromyalgia

One common muscular disorder is fibromyalgia. Fibromyalgia is a chronic condition that causes widespread pain and stiffness throughout the body. The pain is often described as aching or burning and can affect both muscles and joints. People with fibromyalgia may also experience numbness or tingling in their limbs, extreme fatigue, and difficulty sleeping.

What Is Fibromyalgia?

Fibromyalgia is described as a condition characterized by chronic pain and stiffness all over the body. The condition presents a complex set of symptoms including extreme fatigue and sleep disorders, which affect 70% to 80% of patients and result in "non-restorative" sleep meaning patients wake up tired regardless of how long they slept.

Another important characteristic of fibromyalgia is increased sensitivity to external factors such as temperature, pressure, or touch. This condition does not directly damage the muscles, but it affects how the nervous system processes pain signals, making discomfort constant and difficult to manage.

Symptoms

Chronic widespread pain Muscle and joint stiffness Extreme fatigue Non-restorative sleep Numbness in limbs Sensory sensitivity Fibro fog Anxiety and depression Irritable bowel syndrome
  • Physical sensations: Patients experience stiffness in both muscles and joints, as well as numbness in their limbs.
  • Sensory sensitivity: There is a noted sensitivity to external factors such as temperature, pressure, and touch.
  • Energy and sleep: The condition is associated with fatigue and tiredness, along with trouble sleeping.
  • Cognitive difficulties: Often called "fibro fog" impairs the ability to focus, pay attention, and concentrate.
  • Co-occurring conditions: It is common to also suffer from irritable bowel syndrome, anxiety, and depression.

Treatment Options

  1. Pain relievers and anti-inflammatory medication
  2. Antidepressants and anti-seizure medicines
  3. Regular physical activity, yoga and tai chi
  4. Physical therapy and massage therapy
  5. Stress management and healthy sleep habits
  6. Acupuncture and alternative therapies

Hypothesis

If fibromyalgia affects muscle tissue and causes chronic pain and affects sensitivity in the limbs, then we may be able to come up with a temporary treatment that may last in the long run such as pain killers to numb the pain. We can also use exercise to jump-start and give the body something to react to.

Treatment

Treatment for fibromyalgia usually focuses on reducing symptoms rather than curing the condition completely. Common treatments include pain relievers, antidepressants, and anti-seizure medications that help reduce pain sensitivity. Lifestyle changes are also important, such as getting enough sleep, managing stress, and doing regular physical activity. Exercises like stretching, yoga, and tai chi can help reduce stiffness and improve movement. Some patients also benefit from physical therapy, massage therapy, or acupuncture.

03 Robotic Solution

Nano-Robot Vehicle

Our solution requires a nano-bio-robot capable of traveling through the bloodstream via the arteries due to its small size. These nano-bio-robots are used to reach the area of chronic pain and release analgesics in a controlled manner, calming the pain in the affected area and avoiding common analgesics that would numb the entire body instead of just the affected area.

Why the Arteries?

The arteries carry oxygenated blood and nutrients away from the heart toward the muscles and organs. By entering the arterial system, our robot can travel directly to the muscle tissue affected by fibromyalgia and release pain-relief medication precisely where it's needed without affecting the rest of the body. This targeted delivery makes the treatment far more effective than traditional oral analgesics.

Robot Components

  • BullBot Platform: Wheeled robot that navigates the track representing arterial pathways through the body.
  • DC Motors: Control forward, backward, and turning movement through each pathway segment.
  • Servo Motor: Operates a claw mechanism to simulate releasing analgesics at the target muscle location.
  • IR Remote Control: Allows manual control during the RC stage using infrared signal input from the 8Key remote.
  • 8Key Controller (Roborobo): Interface board used for remote input during RC movement stages.
  • ROGIC Board: The brain of the robot receives and executes the programmed sequential actions.
04 Programming

Algorithm and Implementation

Our team used two different programming approaches for this project: a fully automatic sequential program for the Scenario stage, and a remote-control program for the RC stage. Both were built using ROGIC, a visual block-based programming environment for the BullBot robot.

Scenario Sequential Programming

The scenario program runs automatically from start to finish using sequential structures no remote needed. The robot follows a fixed path through the arterial track and releases the analgesics at the target muscle. Every action is coded with DC Motor commands and Delays to control timing precisely.

Scenario Flowchart

▶START
↓DC Motor FF → Move forward · Delay 0.7s
↓DC Motor 00 → Stop · Delay 0.3s
↓DC Motor 0F → Turn · Delay 0.6s
↓DC Motor 00 → Stop · Delay 0.3s
↓DC Motor FF → Move forward · Delay 1.5s
↓DC Motor 00 → Stop · Delay 0.6s
↓DC Motor 0F → Turn · Delay 0.5s
↓DC Motor FF → Move forward · Delay 0.6s
↓DC Motor 00 → Stop · Delay 0.3s
↓Servo → Position 4 (Claw opens analgesics released) · Delay 1.0s
⏹Voice Port 5 → END

RC Remote Control Programming

The RC program uses a While (∞) infinite loop combined with IR R/C infrared buttons. Each button on the 8Key remote triggers a different robot action forward, turn, servo claw, etc. allowing real-time manual control through the track. This simulates a surgeon remotely guiding a nano-robot inside the body.

▶START → While (∞) loop begins
↓IR R/C Button 2 → Voice Port 5 activated → Delay 1.0s → OFF Port 5
↓IR R/C Button 1 → DC Motor FF (Move Forward)
↓IR R/C Button 4 → DC Motor F0 (Turn right)
↓IR R/C Button 3 → DC Motor 0F (Turn left)
↓IR R/C Button 6 → Servo 1 2 (Claw opens analgesics released)
↓IR R/C Button 4 → DC Motor BB (Backward) → DC Motor 00 (Stop)
⏹Loop continues until manually stopped

05 Journey

Stage 3

In Stage 3 we built a physical track representing the circulatory pathways (arteries) of the human body. Our BullBot was programmed sequentially to travel through this track, simulating how a nano-robot would move from the heart through the arteries to reach muscle tissue affected by fibromyalgia and deliver pain relief in the affected area.

The track included intersections and turns representing different vessels, and the robot had to navigate each one precisely using timed DC motor commands and delays.

Scenario Video

RC Remote Control Stage

In the RC phase we reprogrammed the robot to respond to infrared remote control signals using the 8Key controller. Each button was mapped to a specific movement or action, allowing us to manually guide the robot through the arterial pathway in real time. This simulates a surgeon remotely controlling a nano-robot inside the patient's body to deliver treatment exactly where it is needed.
